Building the Sales & Customer Dashboard: A Deep Dive into Business Intelligence with Tableau

Introduction

In this blog post, I'll walk you through a project I created in Tableau: a two-part interactive dashboard focusing on Sales and Customer performance for a fictional retail dataset. This project was designed to simulate the kind of reporting and insights a business would need to improve profitability and customer strategy.

Project Overview

Tools used: Tableau, Excel

Dashboards created:

  • Sales Dashboard
  • Customer Dashboard

Year of Analysis: 2023 vs. 2022 (comparative insights)

Purpose: To provide decision-makers with a clear overview of business performance across sales, customers, and products — all in an interactive, filterable view.

Sales Dashboard – Key Features and Insights

The Sales Dashboard focuses on three main KPIs:

  • Total Sales: $733K (⬆️ 20.4% YoY growth)
  • Total Profit: $93K (⬆️ 12.5% YoY growth)
  • Total Quantity Sold: 12K units (⬆️ 26.8% YoY growth)

These KPIs are visualized across a monthly trend from January to December, allowing the user to easily spot seasonal patterns. The highest and lowest-performing months are highlighted for quick context.

Sales & Profit by Subcategory

A bar chart comparison breaks down 2023 Sales vs. 2022 Sales by product subcategory. This helped me uncover:

  • Top Performers: Phones, Chairs, and Binders
  • Loss Leaders: Envelopes and Fasteners showed high sales but negative profit, flagging potential pricing or cost issues

The color-coding (⬤ Profit / ⬤ Loss) instantly communicates which subcategories are contributing positively or negatively to overall performance.

Sales and Profit Trends Over Time

A dual-line graph displays monthly trends for both sales and profit. Using this, I could identify:

  • Spikes in Q2 and Q4
  • Below-average performance in early Q1
  • Average monthly sales: ~$14K
  • Average monthly profit: ~$2K

This kind of trend analysis is essential for inventory planning, promotional timing, and budget forecasting.

Customer Dashboard – Key Features and Insights

The Customer Dashboard highlights how the business performed from a customer-centric perspective.

Top-Level KPIs:

  • Total Customers: 693 (⬆️ 8.6%)
  • Sales per Customer: $1,058 (⬆️ 10.8%)
  • Total Orders: 1,687 (⬆️ 28.3%)

This indicates not just customer growth, but improved customer value and frequency of purchases.

Customer Distribution by Orders

This histogram breaks down how many times customers purchased:

  • Most customers placed only 1–2 orders
  • A small segment placed 5+ orders, which presents a clear opportunity for loyalty or upsell campaigns

Top 10 Customers by Profit

An insightful table lists the top 10 customers by profit contribution, with details like:

  • Name
  • Last Order Date
  • Total Profit
  • Total Sales
  • Number of Orders

For example:

  • Raymond Buch generated the highest profit: $14,203 from 3 orders
  • Hunter Lopez and Tom Ashbrook also appear as high-value customers with fewer orders but high spend per order

This analysis helps identify potential VIP customers for targeted retention strategies.

Interactivity

Both dashboards are designed with user interactivity in mind. Viewers can:

  • Toggle views for different months
  • Filter by year or product subcategory
  • Dive deep into either profitability trends or customer behavior

These features make the dashboards dynamic decision-making tools, not just static reports.

What I Learned

  • The importance of aligning business goals with data KPIs
  • How to use Tableau's parameters and filters to create multi-view dashboards
  • Storytelling through data: good dashboards don't just show numbers — they guide action

Next Steps

If this were a real-world case, I'd:

  • Recommend re-evaluating pricing or costs for subcategories like Envelopes and Fasteners
  • Develop a customer loyalty program targeting repeat buyers
  • Set up alerts for monthly dips in sales or profit
